# Mitable Montessori — Brandbook

_v1 · drafted 2026-04-30_

This is the single source of truth for how Mitable Montessori looks, sounds, and feels. It is written for everyone who shapes the product: designers, engineers, copywriters, marketers, and founders making cross-cutting calls. If you're deciding between two options and the answer isn't here, the right move is usually to add it here.

The brandbook is organized in cascading order — every later decision flows from the earlier ones. If a foundational principle and a detail conflict, the principle wins.

---

## 1. Brand foundation

### 1.1 What we are

**Mitable Montessori is an AI-assisted record-keeping platform built natively for the Montessori method.**

We help guides spend less time on paperwork and more time with children. We give school leaders organized, trustworthy records. We give parents a quiet, proud window into their child's day.

We are not a generic childcare app. We are not an admin tool that grew a teacher feature. We are a product that speaks Montessori as a first language.

### 1.2 Who we're for (in priority order)

1. **The guide** _(primary)_ — A trained Montessori teacher who works with children every day. They observe constantly, record sparingly, and want their tools to disappear into the work. The brand exists for them first.
2. **The parent** — A family member who wants to feel connected to their child's school day without being intrusive. Often new to Montessori vocabulary; needs gentle translation.
3. **The school admin / lead** — A head of school, director, or coordinator who needs the system to be reliable, organized, and credible. Usually the buyer; rarely the daily user.

When two audiences pull in different directions, the guide wins. Parent and admin surfaces are softened or sharpened variants of the same brand — never separate brands.

### 1.3 Positioning

> **Mitable Montessori is the only record-keeping system built natively for the way Montessori guides actually work.**

Tools like Brightwheel and Transparent Classroom serve childcare and school-ops audiences first; Montessori is bolted on. Mitable starts the other way around: the curriculum, the vocabulary, the rhythm of the work cycle, and the guide's craft are the center. Everything else — privacy, AI, parent updates — is in service of that.

Our supporting pillars (used in marketing, not in the headline):

- **AI-assisted capture.** Voice, photo, or text in. Structured records out.
- **Privacy-first.** Audio and photos never leave the device. Names tokenized before any AI call.
- **Connected.** One observation flows to the parent feed and the admin report without re-entry.

### 1.4 Brand attributes

Four words describe how the product should feel. They sit in deliberate tension — that tension is the brand.

| Attribute        | What it means in practice                                                                            | Where it lives                                               |
| ---------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Warm**         | Cream, terracotta, hand-drawn lines. Feels like a classroom, not a dashboard.                        | Color, illustration, photography, copy.                      |
| **Approachable** | A guide can pick it up on day one. A parent isn't intimidated. Vocabulary is precise but not closed. | Microcopy, onboarding, error states, empty states.           |
| **Playful**      | Small moments of joy. A check that wobbles when it lands. A confetti of paper texture on first save. | Micro-interactions, illustrations, success and empty states. |
| **Unhurried**    | Generous whitespace. Slow, gentle motion. We never rush the user.                                    | Layout, typography scale, motion timing, copy length.        |

**The principle that holds them together:**

> Playful in the moments. Unhurried in the pace.

Joy lives in the small beats — a hand-drawn check, a soft animation when an observation is approved, a warm illustration on an empty state. Calm lives in the structure — generous spacing, slow transitions, copy that doesn't shout. When in doubt: be calm by default, playful when something is worth celebrating.

### 1.5 What we are not

This list is as important as what we are. It defines the things we'll resist when the design pulls toward the obvious SaaS default.

- We are **not** a childcare logging app. We are a record of children's _work_, not their snacks and naps. (Trackers exist, but they are not the center.)
- We are **not** clinical. We don't write like a medical record. The voice is human.
- We are **not** corporate edtech. No "leverage," no "stakeholders," no purple gradients.
- We are **not** a toy. The aesthetic is warm, but the work it supports is serious. We earn the trust of admins and parents through clarity, not by going stiff.
- We are **not** a productivity tool. We don't gamify anything. We don't celebrate streaks. Montessori children aren't optimized.

## 2. Verbal identity

### 2.1 Voice

**One voice across the whole product, with subtle register shifts per audience.**

The Mitable Montessori voice is:

- **Calm.** We never rush. Sentences are short but unhurried. We don't use exclamation points to manufacture energy.
- **Specific.** We name things precisely. A "work cycle" is a work cycle, not a "block." A "guide" is a guide.
- **Warm but not saccharine.** We care, and we show it through specificity, not adjectives. "We saved your observation" beats "Yay! Saved! 🎉"
- **Humble.** The product serves the guide; the guide serves the child. We don't position ourselves as the hero.
- **Quietly confident.** We don't apologize for having opinions. The product knows what a Montessori record should look like.

#### Register shifts by audience

| Surface             | Register                                                     | Example                                                                                    |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Teacher / guide** | Peer-to-peer, present-tense, in-the-moment.                  | "Captured. Want to add another?"                                                           |
| **Parent**          | Narrative, gentle, contextual. Translates terms when needed. | "Today, Ada chose the pink tower during her work cycle (her morning of independent work)." |
| **Admin**           | Clear, organized, slightly more formal.                      | "12 observations are awaiting your review."                                                |

The same voice underneath, dressed for the room.

### 2.2 Voice do / don't

| Do                                                        | Don't                                         |
| --------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| "Saved."                                                  | "Successfully saved! ✅"                      |
| "Ada returned to the binomial cube three days this week." | "Ada CRUSHED the binomial cube this week! 🎉" |
| "Take a breath. We've got this draft."                    | "Don't worry! We've got you covered!"         |
| "Three observations need your review."                    | "🚨 You have 3 pending observations!"         |
| "The work cycle has ended."                               | "Time's up!"                                  |
| "Couldn't save. Check your connection and try again."     | "Oops! Something went wrong 😬"               |
| "Nothing here yet."                                       | "No data found."                              |

### 2.3 Vocabulary

**Default to Montessori-native vocabulary.** It is the most direct expression of our positioning. Where a parent or new user might not know a term, we translate inline rather than abandoning it.

#### Terms we use natively

| We say                   | Not                         | Notes                                                                               |
| ------------------------ | --------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Guide**                | Teacher                     | Use "teacher" only in marketing copy where SEO/clarity demands it.                  |
| **Work**                 | Activity, task, lesson      | "Lesson" is reserved for a guide-led presentation specifically.                     |
| **Work cycle**           | Class period, block         |                                                                                     |
| **Prepared environment** | Classroom layout            | Used internally and on admin surfaces; rare on parent surfaces.                     |
| **Sensitive period**     | Developmental window        | Used carefully; explained when shown to parents.                                    |
| **Presentation**         | Demo, tutorial, lesson plan | A guide _gives_ a presentation; a child does the work.                              |
| **Observation**          | Note, log entry             | The atomic unit of the record.                                                      |
| **Child**                | Kid, kiddo, student         | Always "child." Never "kiddo." Never "student" (used only in admin/legal contexts). |
| **Family**               | Parents, guardians          | Inclusive of any adult connected to the child.                                      |
| **Conference report**    | Report card                 | We don't grade.                                                                     |
| **Material**             | Toy, manipulative, tool     | Specific to Montessori work materials.                                              |
| **Plane of development** | Age group, grade            | Reserved for curriculum context.                                                    |

#### Words we avoid

- **Kiddos, lil ones, little learners.** Sentimental shorthand. We use "children."
- **Crush, smash, win, level up, streak, badge, points.** Gamification language is incompatible with Montessori.
- **Stakeholders, end users, leverage, robust, seamless, world-class.** Corporate edtech vocabulary.
- **Awesome, amazing, super, ultra.** Manufactured enthusiasm.
- **Onboard (as verb for children), users, accounts.** When talking about families, we say "added to the classroom," "joined the school," "Ada's family."
- **AI, ML, model, prompt.** The technology is invisible to teachers and parents. Admins may see it referenced, but always in service of an outcome ("drafted by Mitable" rather than "GPT-generated").

### 2.4 Microcopy principles

1. **Lead with the verb.** "Save observation," not "Click here to save your observation."
2. **One sentence, then a second only if needed.** Most tooltips and error states should fit in one breath.
3. **Specific over general.** "Couldn't reach the server" beats "Network error." "Ada has 3 unmatched observations from yesterday" beats "You have unsynced data."
4. **Empty states are an invitation.** Never "No data." Always a gentle "Nothing here yet — when you capture an observation, it'll appear here." Often paired with a small spot illustration.
5. **Errors don't blame the user.** Never "You forgot to..." Always "We need..." or "This needs..."
6. **Confirmation doesn't celebrate.** A saved observation is a saved observation. A drafted report is a drafted report. We don't throw confetti at routine moments.
7. **No emoji in product copy.** Emoji is a visual language; ours is hand-drawn marks. (Marketing copy may use them sparingly.)

### 2.5 Voice cheatsheet for common surfaces

| Surface                  | Voice example                                                                                  |
| ------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Capture confirmation** | "Captured. _Add another_."                                                                     |
| **Sync error**           | "Saved on this device. We'll sync as soon as you're back online."                              |
| **Empty roster**         | "Your roster will appear here once your school is set up. _Talk to your admin_."               |
| **Parent feed empty**    | "No updates yet today. Ada's guide will share moments as they come."                           |
| **Admin review queue**   | "12 observations are ready for your review."                                                   |
| **Report drafted**       | "Draft ready. Take your time — review when you have a moment."                                 |
| **Onboarding done**      | "You're set up. The first work cycle is the hardest. We'll be here."                           |
| **Permission denied**    | "This is reserved for your school's admin. _Ask them to invite you_, or get in touch with us." |

## 3. Visual identity

### 3.1 Logo

The wordmark is **hand-lettered "Mitable"** with a small accompanying symbol. "Montessori" sits below or beside as a clean, calm sub-label in the body typeface.

The logo sets the ceiling for hand-drawnness across the rest of the brand. Illustrations and decorative marks should match — never exceed — the logo's level of imperfection. A tightly hand-drawn logo with wildly sketchy illustrations reads inconsistent.

#### Construction notes (for the designer)

- **Wordmark:** Custom hand-lettering or near-custom (Caveat Brush, Patrick Hand SC, or commissioned). Letter weights should vary subtly — like a careful pencil, not a marker.
- **Symbol:** A small, simple mark that complements the wordmark. Candidates to explore in design: a single leaf, a circle drawn as a child might draw it (open, slightly imperfect), a simple sun, a hand. _Avoid:_ literal Montessori material renderings (pink tower, etc.) — they age poorly and feel like clip art.
- **Sub-label:** "Montessori" set in the body sans (DM Sans or Inter), tracked +40, all caps or sentence case.
- **Lockup variants needed:** horizontal (web header), stacked (favicon, app icon), symbol-only (small spaces, social avatars).

#### Clear space

Minimum clear space around the logo is the height of the lowercase "m" in "Mitable." Don't crowd it.

#### Misuse

Don't: stretch, recolor outside the palette, drop on busy photography, place on pure white (#FFFFFF), animate with bouncy springs (the logo is unhurried), or pair with another sans-serif for the wordmark (the hand-lettering is the point).

### 3.2 Color

The palette is **earthy classroom** — warm cream backgrounds with accents drawn from natural Montessori materials: terracotta clay, sage cloth, butter wood, dusty blue glass.

#### Core palette

| Token                  | Value     | Use                                             |
| ---------------------- | --------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| `color.bg.canvas`      | `#FAF6EE` | Page background (warm cream, never pure white). |
| `color.bg.surface`     | `#FFFBF3` | Cards, modals, elevated surfaces.               |
| `color.bg.muted`       | `#F2EDE0` | Secondary surfaces, sidebars.                   |
| `color.ink.primary`    | `#2A2723` | Body text, headings.                            |
| `color.ink.secondary`  | `#5C544A` | Secondary text, metadata.                       |
| `color.ink.muted`      | `#9C9285` | Placeholders, disabled.                         |
| `color.border.default` | `#E0D8C8` | Standard borders, dividers.                     |
| `color.border.strong`  | `#B89880` | Emphasis borders, hand-drawn frame accents.     |

#### Accent palette

Each accent has a primary tone (for fills and CTAs) and a soft tone (for backgrounds and tints).

| Token                     | Primary   | Soft      | Meaning                                           |
| ------------------------- | --------- | --------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| `color.accent.terracotta` | `#C46A4F` | `#F4DDD2` | Primary CTAs, warmth, "the brand color."          |
| `color.accent.sage`       | `#7A9B7A` | `#DDE7DA` | Secondary, growth, success.                       |
| `color.accent.butter`     | `#E8C56A` | `#F6E8B8` | Highlights, joy, celebrations.                    |
| `color.accent.dusty-blue` | `#6B8AA6` | `#D2DCE5` | Links, info, parent-side surfaces.                |
| `color.accent.clay`       | `#B89880` | `#E5D8C8` | Subtle dividers, paper accents, hand-drawn marks. |

#### Semantic tokens

| Token                  | Value (resolves to)         | Use                                |
| ---------------------- | --------------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| `color.action.primary` | `color.accent.terracotta`   | Primary CTA fill.                  |
| `color.action.success` | `color.accent.sage`         | Success, approved, synced.         |
| `color.action.info`    | `color.accent.dusty-blue`   | Info, links, parent surfaces.      |
| `color.action.warning` | `color.accent.butter`       | Soft warnings (review, attention). |
| `color.action.danger`  | `#A24A3D` (deep terracotta) | Destructive only. Used sparingly.  |

#### Usage rules

- **Backgrounds are always cream.** Never pure white (`#FFFFFF`). White feels clinical and breaks the brand instantly. If a third-party component forces white, wrap it.
- **One hero color per surface.** Terracotta is the brand color. Sage is the supporting voice. Butter is rare and joyful. Don't paint a screen in five accents — pick one or two.
- **Accents are surfaces and ink, not decoration.** Use them with purpose: terracotta on a CTA means "this is the action," not "this looks nice here."
- **The dusty blue belongs to the parent surface.** It's the only accent that's slightly cooler — it gives the parent feed a calmer, more reflective register without breaking the family of the palette.
- **Accessibility:** all ink-on-cream and accent-on-soft combinations meet WCAG AA at body sizes. Terracotta on cream meets AA at large sizes; for body text, drop to deeper terracotta `#A24A3D` or use ink.

### 3.3 Typography

A two-typeface system. Display is hand-lettered for emotional moments. Body is a calm, humanist sans for everything else.

| Token          | Family                   | Use                                                                                                   |
| -------------- | ------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| `font.display` | Caveat (Google Fonts)    | Marketing hero, logo, occasional UI accents (empty-state titles, celebration copy). _Never_ for body. |
| `font.body`    | DM Sans (Google Fonts)   | All UI text, body copy, headings inside the product.                                                  |
| `font.numeric` | DM Sans, tabular figures | Numbers, dates, counts. Always tabular for alignment.                                                 |

> **Alternates if licensing or rendering issues arise:** Patrick Hand for display; Inter for body. Stick with one pairing across the product.

#### Type scale (body)

A calm, generous scale. Don't shrink below 14px in the product.

| Token             | Size | Line height | Weight      | Use                                            |
| ----------------- | ---- | ----------- | ----------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| `text.display.lg` | 48px | 1.1         | 400 Caveat  | Marketing hero only.                           |
| `text.display.md` | 32px | 1.15        | 400 Caveat  | Empty-state title, celebration moments.        |
| `text.heading.xl` | 28px | 1.25        | 600 DM Sans | Page titles.                                   |
| `text.heading.lg` | 22px | 1.3         | 600 DM Sans | Section titles.                                |
| `text.heading.md` | 18px | 1.4         | 600 DM Sans | Card titles, dialog titles.                    |
| `text.body.lg`    | 17px | 1.55        | 400 DM Sans | Comfortable reading, parent feed narratives.   |
| `text.body.md`    | 15px | 1.55        | 400 DM Sans | Default body text.                             |
| `text.body.sm`    | 13px | 1.5         | 400 DM Sans | Metadata, captions, secondary info.            |
| `text.label`      | 12px | 1.4         | 500 DM Sans | Tags, labels, all-caps treatments (track +60). |

#### Typography rules

- **Display font is rare and earned.** Use it where the moment is emotional or the surface is marketing. Never in dense data UI.
- **Line length:** 60–75 characters max on body copy. Parent narratives can be longer (up to 80) because they're written in prose.
- **Tracking:** body type stays at default. All-caps labels track +60. Display tracking is loose (+20).
- **Weights:** body uses 400 and 600 only. No light weights (they break warmth). No 700+ (too aggressive).
- **No italics in UI.** Italics in DM Sans look stiff. For emphasis, use weight or color.

### 3.4 Iconography

Icons are warm, calm, and consistent.

- **Library:** Lucide at `strokeWidth={1.5}` with rounded line caps.
- **Sizes:** 16px (inline), 20px (default), 24px (emphasis). Always pixel-aligned.
- **Color:** ink primary or ink secondary. Accent-colored icons are reserved for action states (terracotta = active, sage = success).
- **Pairing with type:** an icon next to text aligns to the cap height of the body, not the baseline.

> **Avoid:** filled icons (too heavy), iconography with personality of its own (Mona, custom illustrative icon sets) — they fight the hand-drawn marks.

### 3.5 Hand-drawn marks

The brand's signature visual element. Used with restraint — they are seasoning, not the dish.

| Mark                     | Use                                                                         |
| ------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Hand-drawn underline** | Emphasis on display headings, highlighting a key word in narrative copy.    |
| **Hand-drawn check**     | Approval moments — a confirmed observation, an approved report.             |
| **Hand-drawn arrow**     | Inline pointers, "next step" indicators, marketing flow diagrams.           |
| **Hand-drawn frame**     | Wrapping a featured photo or quote on marketing pages. Optional in product. |
| **Hand-drawn divider**   | Section dividers in long content (parent feed day breaks).                  |

Implementation: **Rough Notation** library for runtime-animated marks (highlights, underlines that draw on first view); static SVGs for marks that need to be perfect every time (logo accent, marketing scenes).

Rules:

- One or two marks per screen, not five.
- Always in clay (`#B89880`) or terracotta (`#C46A4F`) — never in black or pure accent fills.
- Marks animate on first render (300–500ms draw-on), never on hover or repeat.
- In dense data UI (roster grid, review queue), marks are absent. They live in the emotional surfaces.

### 3.6 Illustration

Small spot illustrations — not full editorial scenes. Targeting **8–12 spot illustrations for v1**.

#### Style

- Hand-drawn line work with soft fills from the accent palette.
- Slightly imperfect — wobbly lines, off-center fills (color "leaking" outside the lines like a child's drawing). Done deliberately, not as a crutch.
- Children, when shown, are abstract — no specific facial features, no skin tone (the illustrations represent any child). When children must be specific, use real photography submitted by the school.
- Materials and environment are recognizable but stylized (a "pink tower" reads as a tower of squares, not a precise rendering).

#### v1 illustration set

1. **Empty roster** — A guide standing in a bright classroom, looking toward the door.
2. **First observation captured** — A small celebration mark with a hand-drawn check.
3. **Parent feed empty** — A child reading on a soft mat, light through a window.
4. **Report drafted** — A clipboard with a hand-drawn checkmark, pen resting nearby.
5. **Onboarding welcome** — A prepared environment, viewed through a doorway.
6. **Sync error / offline** — A small leaf, blowing in wind. (Calm, not alarmed.)
7. **Permission denied** — A door, gently closed.
8. **Marketing hero** — A guide observing a child at work, in profile, far enough away to be respectful.

Plus 2–4 reusable secondary spots for section dividers and marketing pages.

#### Photography (when used)

Photography is rare — only in marketing, never in product UI. When used:

- Real schools and real moments. No stock photography. No staged smiles.
- Warm, natural light. Cream and wood tones; avoid cold fluorescents.
- Children are shown at work, not posed. Faces often partially obscured (back of head, hands, profile) for both privacy and quietness.
- Photos sit in hand-drawn frames or are softened with a slight cream tone overlay.

### 3.7 Motion

Motion is **slow, gentle, and rare.** We never bounce, snap, or surprise.

| Token                | Duration | Easing                                      | Use                                                  |
| -------------------- | -------- | ------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| `motion.fast`        | 200ms    | `ease-out`                                  | Hover states, focus rings, small toggles.            |
| `motion.medium`      | 350ms    | `cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1)`              | Default — modals, drawers, page transitions.         |
| `motion.slow`        | 500ms    | `cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1)`              | Hand-drawn marks drawing on, illustrations entering. |
| `motion.celebration` | 600ms    | gentle spring (low stiffness, high damping) | Reserved for approval moments. Soft, not bouncy.     |

Rules:

- **No spring physics by default.** Use eased curves. Springs are reserved for celebration moments and even then they are low-energy.
- **No animations on data tables, grids, or dense lists.** Calm motion in calm places, no motion in functional ones.
- **First-render only for delight.** A hand-drawn underline draws on once; it doesn't redraw on scroll.
- **Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.** All animations have a reduced-motion fallback (opacity-only, no transforms).

### 3.8 Texture

A **subtle paper texture** sits behind cream surfaces — barely visible, but felt.

- Implementation: low-opacity (~3%) SVG noise layered as a Tailwind utility (`.bg-paper`).
- Used on: page backgrounds, hero marketing surfaces.
- Not used on: dense data UI, small components, anywhere it would compete with content.

### 3.9 Spacing & shape

#### Spacing scale (4px base)

```
4   8   12   16   24   32   48   64   96
```

Generous spacing is part of "unhurried." Default to one step larger than feels necessary, especially on parent-facing surfaces.

#### Radius

Soft, rounded — never hard corners.

| Token         | Value  | Use                                   |
| ------------- | ------ | ------------------------------------- |
| `radius.sm`   | 6px    | Small buttons, tags, inline pills.    |
| `radius.md`   | 10px   | Default — inputs, small cards.        |
| `radius.lg`   | 16px   | Cards, modals, larger surfaces.       |
| `radius.xl`   | 24px   | Hero illustrations, marketing panels. |
| `radius.full` | 9999px | Avatars, pill buttons, status dots.   |

#### Shadows

Soft, slightly offset, never harsh. Like a sticker on paper.

| Token         | Value                                 | Use                                            |
| ------------- | ------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| `shadow.sm`   | `0 1px 2px rgba(42, 39, 35, 0.06)`    | Subtle elevation.                              |
| `shadow.md`   | `0 4px 12px rgba(42, 39, 35, 0.08)`   | Default cards, dropdowns.                      |
| `shadow.lg`   | `0 12px 32px rgba(42, 39, 35, 0.10)`  | Modals, popovers.                              |
| `shadow.warm` | `0 4px 16px rgba(196, 106, 79, 0.15)` | Reserved — emphasis only (e.g. selected card). |

## 4. Application

How the brand shows up across the product surfaces defined in `routes.md`.

### 4.1 Teacher app (`/app/*`) — primary surface

The brand expresses itself most fully here. The guide is in the work, often on a phone, often one-handed.

**Design priorities:**

- Single-handed thumb reach. Primary actions in the lower third.
- Capture (`/app/quick-add`) feels like opening a notebook, not opening a form. Cream paper, hand-drawn frame, big quiet textarea.
- Voice capture is the default; the mic button is large, terracotta, with a slow, breathing animation when listening.
- Roster (`/app/roster`) is dense but warm — small avatars (or initials in clay tones), generous row spacing, no zebra striping.
- Child profiles (`/app/roster/:childId`) feel like a portfolio page, not a CRM record.

**Tone:** "Captured." "Saved." "Want to add another?"

### 4.2 Parent surface (`/family/*`) — emotional surface

The parent feed is the most emotional surface in the product. Lean into warmth. Display font appears here more often than anywhere else.

**Design priorities:**

- Day-by-day narrative cards, not a stream of timestamps.
- Photos are large, warm, in hand-drawn frames.
- Dusty blue accents (the parent-surface color).
- Glossary tooltips on Montessori terms — gentle inline translation, no separate FAQ to hunt for.

**Tone:** "Today, Ada returned to the binomial cube — a work she's been drawn to all week."

### 4.3 Admin surface (`/admin/*`) — credible surface

The buyer needs to feel that this is a serious tool. Slightly tighter, slightly more functional, but unmistakably the same brand.

**Design priorities:**

- Denser tables and lists (still spacious by SaaS standards, dense by Mitable standards).
- Fewer hand-drawn marks. Marks live in success states and approvals, not in routine UI.
- More numbers and metadata visible at a glance.
- The admin AI agent (`/api/admin/agent`) renders its responses in body sans, not display — it's a serious assistant, not a chatbot.

**Tone:** "12 observations are awaiting your review. 3 reports are in draft."

### 4.4 Marketing site (`/`, `/features`, `/for-teachers`, `/for-schools`)

The marketing site is where the brand is loudest. Display font in heroes, illustrations on every section, hand-drawn marks throughout.

**Design priorities:**

- A single hero spot illustration per page (the largest art in the product).
- One quote, hand-framed, per page.
- `/for-teachers` leans warm and present-tense; `/for-schools` leans organized and credible. Same brand, two registers.
- The pricing page is calm and confident. No "Most popular!" badges. No countdowns.

### 4.5 System emails

System emails (welcome, invite, weekly digest, report-ready) are written in body voice and lightly designed.

- Cream background, ink primary text, one hand-drawn underline on the heading, terracotta CTA button.
- No images in transactional emails (deliverability + privacy). Marketing emails may include one spot illustration.
- Subject lines are calm: "Your weekly digest" not "Don't miss this week!"

### 4.6 Conference reports (printable PDF)

Reports are the most formal artifact. The brand is quiet here but still present.

- Cream background even when printed.
- Display font on the cover only — child name, period, school.
- Body sans throughout the narrative.
- One small hand-drawn divider between sections.
- The footer carries the school logo; the Mitable wordmark is a tiny acknowledgment, not a watermark.

### 4.7 App store / PWA install assets

- App icon: the symbol-only logo on a cream background, in a soft squircle.
- Splash screen: cream, with the wordmark centered and a single hand-drawn underline that draws on as the app loads.
- Screenshots: prefer the parent feed and the capture modal — the most emotionally resonant surfaces.

## 5. Design system handoff

This section is for engineering. Tokens here are intended to land directly in `tailwind.config.ts` and in shadcn/ui theme overrides.

### 5.1 Recommended libraries

| Layer                 | Choice                               | Why                                                                                |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Headless primitives   | **Radix Primitives** (re-styled)     | shadcn defaults are too modern/SaaS. Use Radix raw and apply our own visual layer. |
| Hand-drawn marks      | **Rough Notation**                   | Lightweight (~9KB), runtime-animated underlines/highlights/checks.                 |
| Static decorative SVG | Custom-drawn SVGs in `public/marks/` | For marks that need to be exact (logo accent, marketing illustrations).            |
| Icons                 | **Lucide** at `strokeWidth={1.5}`    | Calm, consistent line work. Avoid filled variants.                                 |

| Body font             | **DM Sans** (Google Fonts)           | Humanist sans, calm, excellent at small sizes, tabular numerals available.         |
| Motion                | **Framer Motion**                    | Eased curves, gentle springs, `prefers-reduced-motion` aware.                      |

### 5.3 Component principles

When building shared components:

1. **Default to spacious.** If padding or margin feels right, add 4px more.
2. **Inputs sit on `surface`, not on `canvas`.** Cards and inputs need the slight contrast.
3. **Borders before shadows.** A 1px clay border feels warmer than a shadow alone.
4. **CTAs are terracotta, single-color, no gradients.**
5. **Disabled states are at 50% opacity, never gray.** Gray breaks the warm palette.
6. **Focus rings are sage** (`color.sage` at 2px outline + 2px offset), not blue.

### 5.4 Accessibility

- All foreground/background combinations meet WCAG AA. Body text on cream backgrounds is `ink.primary` (`#2A2723`).
- Terracotta `#C46A4F` on cream meets AA at 18px+/bold or use `terracotta.deep` (`#A24A3D`) for body-sized terracotta text.
- Focus states are always visible — sage 2px outline with 2px offset.
- Motion respects `prefers-reduced-motion`: replace transforms with opacity transitions and skip celebration springs entirely.
- Hand-drawn marks (Rough Notation) have a `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback that renders the mark instantly without the draw-on animation.
- Display font (Caveat) is decorative — never load-bearing for meaning. Body sans always provides the accessible reading.

### 5.5 Do / don't quick reference

| Do                                      | Don't                            |
| --------------------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Cream background everywhere             | Pure white anywhere              |
| Terracotta CTA, single color            | Gradient buttons, glossy buttons |
| Eased curves, 350ms default             | Bouncy springs everywhere        |
| Hand-drawn marks on emotional moments   | Hand-drawn marks on data tables  |
| Display font in heroes and empty states | Display font in body or labels   |
| Lucide icons at `strokeWidth={1.5}`     | Filled icons, mixed icon sets    |
| `guide`, `work`, `child`                | `teacher`, `activity`, `kiddo`   |
| "Saved."                                | "Successfully saved! 🎉"         |
| Generous whitespace                     | Dense, productivity-tool layouts |
| One accent per surface                  | Five accents on one screen       |

## 6. Out of scope for v1

These are intentionally deferred. They have a home here so they can be added without restructuring the brandbook.

- **Dark mode.** The earthy palette is built for light. Dark mode requires its own palette derivation and isn't a v1 priority.
- **Sub-brands per school.** Mitable Montessori is one brand for v1. School branding (logos, colors) lives on the school's profile page, not in the product chrome.
- **Physical brand assets.** Business cards, swag, signage. Will be addressed when the product reaches a stage where they're needed.
- **Localization beyond English.** Voice and vocabulary translations to other languages are out of scope. The Montessori-native vocabulary will need careful translation when the time comes (some terms, like "guide" and "work cycle," have established translations in major Montessori-speaking languages).
- **Marketing video and motion identity.** The brandbook covers product motion. A separate video/motion identity (broadcast logo animation, OOH motion) will follow if and when it's needed.
