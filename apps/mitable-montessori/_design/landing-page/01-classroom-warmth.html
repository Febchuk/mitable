What it does

Built around the work — not the worksheet.

Three quiet tools that respect how Montessori classrooms actually run. No pop-ups. No notification spam. No gamification.

i.

The whole-child timeline

Every child has a single, calm timeline — observations, lessons given, materials mastered. Pull it up in the morning, add a note in 8 seconds, close it. That's the whole loop.

ii.

Curriculum, the AMI way

The full Montessori curriculum tree — practical life, sensorial, language, math, cultural — already mapped. Tap a lesson to mark presented, practiced, or mastered.

iii.

Reports that read like reports

Term reports written in the same warm, observational voice Montessori has always used — auto-drafted from the year's records, then yours to edit, sign, and send.

~ in the classroom

Capture an observation in the time it takes to tie a shoe.

Tap the child. Tap the material. Voice-note or type. Done. Mitable handles the timestamp, the curriculum link, and the spider sheet so you can keep your eyes on the work.

  • Voice or text — your choice, every time
  • Auto-tags to the AMI curriculum tree
  • Works offline; syncs when you reach Wi-Fi
~ across the year

Curriculum coverage, at a glance — and at a depth.

The curriculum tree shows what each child has been presented, what they're practicing, and what they've made their own. Color-restrained, so the eye can rest, and the guide can plan.

  • Presented · Practiced · Mastered states
  • Filter by subject, age, or work cycle
  • Print-ready for parent conferences
